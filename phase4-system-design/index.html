Phase 4 — System Design

27 tutorials · HLD in depth + frontend system design + low-level design · calibrated for both tracks — FAANG-grade distributed design and enterprise/fintech loops (Optum, JPMC, Goldman & similar)

You will face one of two different rounds — prepare for both. A frontend or full-stack loop at a product company gives you a frontend system design round: design a feed UI, an autocomplete, a design system. A backend or full-stack loop gives you the classic distributed round: design a URL shortener, a chat system, a rate limiter. Enterprise and fintech loops skew pragmatic — payments, idempotency, inventory contention, schema and caching — not web-scale trivia. Your résumé will get you into all of them, so this phase covers all of them.

Case studies teach archetypes, not answers. There are only about a dozen distinct shapes in distributed design, and every interview question is one of them wearing a costume. Design a URL shortener and you've learned read-heavy key-value systems; design Ticketmaster and you've learned inventory under contention. Each case study below is a worked transcript of a strong 45-minute answer — requirements, estimation with real numbers, API, schema, architecture, then the deep-dive the interviewer actually pushes into, plus the trade-offs and follow-ups. Read the foundations first. The case studies assume that vocabulary and will feel like memorization without it.

The single highest-leverage habit in this phase: say the trade-off before the interviewer asks for it. Junior candidates present a design; senior candidates present a design, name what it costs, and say what they'd do differently at 10× the scale.
