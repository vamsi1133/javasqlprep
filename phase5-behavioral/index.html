Phase 5 — Behavioral & Mock Interviews

8 tutorials · the round that most often decides the offer once your coding is good enough · STAR discipline + a reusable story bank + company calibration for both tracks — FAANG-grade values loops (Amazon LPs, Meta, Google) and enterprise/fintech behavioral (Optum, JPMC, Goldman & similar)

This is the round people under-prepare and lose on. You are strong technically — the behavioral round is where a 7-YOE candidate either reads as a senior engineer who owns outcomes, or as a mid-level engineer who did tasks. The difference is almost never the raw material; it's structure, specificity, and knowing what each question is actually testing. This phase turns your seven years of real work into eight to ten reusable, quantified stories you can deliver in two minutes without rambling, and drills the delivery until it's automatic.

You will face two flavours — prepare for both. FAANG and big-tech run a values-calibrated behavioral round graded against a published rubric — Amazon's Leadership Principles are the extreme case, with a dedicated Bar Raiser probing depth and data. Enterprise and fintech loops (Optum, JPMC, Goldman) skew pragmatic: ownership under regulation, working across siloed teams, handling production incidents and compliance, delivering under ambiguity — less rubric theatre, more "can we trust you with the system." The same story bank serves both; you just re-aim the framing. Every page below teaches the archetype and then gives you the exact sentences to say out loud.

Block A — Method & material

  • 01The Behavioral Round: what's actually graded What the interviewer is really scoring (signal, not the story) · STAR and the SAR/STARL variants · how much time each part gets · the 2-minute answer shape · leading with impact · quantifying everything · first-person "I" not "we" · past behaviour vs hypotheticals · how 7-YOE answers differ from 3-YOE · the note-taking rubric interviewers actually fill in
  • 02Build Your Story Bank The highest-leverage prep in the phase. Mine 7 years for 8–10 reusable stories that each cover multiple themes · the story-bank matrix (story × theme) · how to pick projects with conflict, ambiguity and measurable impact · reconstructing metrics you didn't track · the one-page cheat-sheet format · rehearsing out loud without memorizing a script

Block B — The question archetypes

  • 03Leadership, Ownership & Influence "Tell me about a time you drove a project / took initiative / led without authority / disagreed and committed." What the interviewer probes for · influence without a title (the frontend-lead-in-practice angle) · ownership beyond your ticket · 2–3 fully-worked STAR stories · the follow-up drilling ("what would you do differently?") · mapping to Amazon Ownership / Deliver Results
  • 04Conflict, Feedback & Collaboration "A time you disagreed with your manager / a peer / had a difficult teammate / received hard feedback." The trap (badmouthing, being the hero, no resolution) · disagreeing with data and staying respectful · cross-functional friction with PM/design/backend · 2–3 worked stories · giving and receiving feedback · mapping to Have Backbone; Disagree & Commit / Earn Trust
  • 05Failure, Mistakes & Ambiguity "Biggest failure / a mistake you made / missed a deadline / vague requirements / a production incident." Picking a real failure that isn't disqualifying · owning it without deflecting · the learning-and-what-changed close (the part most people skip) · operating with no clear spec · 2–3 worked stories · mapping to Dive Deep / Learn & Be Curious / Bias for Action
  • 06"Tell Me About Yourself", Why-Company & Questions to Ask The open and the close that bookend every loop. A 90-second positioning pitch for a frontend-expert pivoting to full-stack (without over-claiming Java YOE) · "why this company / why now / why this role" that isn't generic · the strong questions to ask them that read as senior · handling "what's your biggest weakness" and gaps/job-hops · red flags to avoid

Block C — Company calibration

  • 07Company Calibration: LPs, Values & the Fintech Bar Re-aiming the same stories per company. All 16 Amazon Leadership Principles with the question each generates and which of your stories to fire · the Bar Raiser · Meta (Move Fast, signals) and Google (googliness, general cognitive) at awareness level · the enterprise/fintech behavioral bar — ownership under regulation, incident response, working across silos, compliance and risk mindset (Optum, JPMC, Goldman) · a story-to-value lookup table

Block D — Execution

  • 08Mocks, Delivery & Salary Negotiation Turning prep into performance. Delivery mechanics — pacing, filler words, structure signposting, recovering from a blank · a 40-question mock bank grouped by theme, click-to-reveal, with what each is testing · self-run and peer mock protocols · the red-flag checklist interviewers use · then salary negotiation — the number-first rule, competing offers, total comp vs base, levelling, and the exact lines to say (and never say)

The single highest-leverage habit in this phase: rehearse out loud, not in your head. A story that feels crisp when you think it collapses into rambling the first time you say it. Build the bank in Phase 5, page 02, then say every answer aloud and time it to two minutes. That gap — between the story in your head and the story out of your mouth — is the entire round.
